I am an Assistant Professor of Economics at Wellesley College.

Previously, I was a Senior Research Economist at the Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas.

My research builds on models of frictional markets with sound micro-foundations. Until now, I have studied their application to monetary theory, over-the-counter financial markets, and labor markets.

I hold a B.A. in Social Sciences and a M.A. in Economics and Public Policy from Sciences Po Paris (France), and a Ph.D. in Economics from the University of California, Irvine.
