About me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at the University of California, Irvine and currently on the job market.

I am broadly interested in models of frictional markets with sound micro-foundations. Until now, I have studied their application to monetary theory, over-the-counter financial markets, labor markets, and more recently, to the spread of a virus through social interactions.

My research on the impact of payment and inventory constraints on the participation of investors to over-the-counter markets was recently published in the Journal of Economic Theory.

In my spare time, I enjoy road biking along the coast and in the moutains of Los Angeles. And though a French native, living in Southern California has turned me into quite a baseball fan. I love catching a game at Dodger stadium whenever I can.
